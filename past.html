Physics Research

My doctoral work in quantum condensed matter theory identified new types of quantum and classical behavior and new routes towards the construction of quantum phases. I worked on many-body localization (3), topological phases (3, 4), non-Fermi liquids (2), and topologically protected mechanical systems (1).

Journal Publications

[1]. Phonon analog of topological nodal semimetals."
Hoi Chun Po, Yasaman Bahri, and Ashvin Vishwanath.
Phys. Rev. B. 93, 205158 (2016) (Editor's Suggestion)

  • We presented a construction of the first topologically protected mechanical system that is gapless in its bulk.

[2]. “Stable non-Fermi-liquid phase of itinerant spin-orbit coupled ferromagnets.”
Yasaman Bahri and Andrew C. Potter.
Phys. Rev. B 92, 035131 (2015).

  • We discussed a simple construction of a metal coupled to Goldstone bosons that, unlike most other cases, gives rise to a non-Fermi rather than a Fermi liquid. The ingredients are a ferromagnetic metal with broken continuous rotation symmetry and spin-orbit coupling. We showed within a combined N, epsilon expansion that the non-Fermi liquid is expected to be robust to the relevant instabilities (superconducting and spin-density wave orders). This gave one of the few known examples of a phase of coupled fermions and bosons where the non-Fermi liquid would not be masked at low temperatures.

[3]. “Localization and topology protected quantum coherence at the edge of hot matter.”
Yasaman Bahri, Ronen Vosk, Ehud Altman, and Ashvin Vishwanath.
Nature Communications 6:7341 (2015).

  • We showed for the first time that many-body localization and symmetry-protected topological order together can give rise to quantum coherent dynamics at the edge of a many-body system.

[4]. “Detecting Majorana fermions in quasi-one-dimensional topological phases using nonlocal order parameters.”
Yasaman Bahri and Ashvin Vishwanath.
Phys. Rev. B 89, 155135 (2014).

  • We devised nonlocal order parameters (measurements requiring an extensive number of operators) for certain classes of low-dimensional symmetry-protected topological phases of fermions. The motivation was to have order parameters that are simple and potentially measurable with new experimental probes, e.g. a quantum gas microscope.

My undergraduate senior honors thesis investigated a scaling theory for 1D spin systems near a quantum critical point that combined both the effects of finite size and finite entanglement due to the limitations of computational representation by tensors networks.
